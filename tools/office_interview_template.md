# Gov-office tacit-knowledge interview template

**Purpose**: capture the practical, navigational, and procedural knowledge that gov.np never publishes — room numbers, document quirks, queue patterns, parking, common reasons people get sent back. This template guides a 30-minute structured interview with someone who knows the office.

**Who to interview** — for each office, ideally **three perspectives** (triangulate):
1. A counter officer or service-desk staff member
2. An experienced local "agent" or consultant who handles cases at this office regularly
3. A long-time security guard or receptionist (often knows building layout best)

If only one is available, prefer the experienced agent — they see the most cases and have less institutional defensiveness than officers.

**Recording format**:
- Audio (smartphone or any recorder is fine; aim for clear voice, low background noise)
- Pre-interview consent statement (read at start, see Appendix A)
- One audio file per interviewee
- Filename: `<YYYY-MM-DD>_<office_slug>_<role>_<initials>.wav`
  - e.g. `2026-04-30_jirimun_officer_RP.wav`
- Optional: photos at key moments (see PART 11)

**Interview language**: whichever language the interviewee prefers (Devanagari Nepali, Roman-Nepali, English, code-mixed). The ASR pipeline handles all three.

**Time budget**: 30 minutes is the target. Some PARTs will be quick. If interviewee is talkative, let them go — even off-topic stories often contain useful tacit info.

---

## Pre-interview (read aloud, ~30 sec)

> "Hello, my name is [name]. I'm working on a project to help citizens navigate Nepal-government services more easily. We're collecting practical information about how this office actually works — things like which counter to visit first, what documents people forget to bring, the best time of day to come.
>
> I'd like to record our conversation so I can transcribe it later. Your name and any colleagues' names will not be used in the final system — we'll only keep the practical information. You can stop the recording any time, and I can remove anything you don't want included.
>
> Do you agree to be recorded?"

If yes → start recording, restate consent on tape, proceed. If no → take notes by hand, mark the transcript "no-recording".

---

## PART 1 — Office identification (3 min)

1. What is the official name of this office?
2. What do most citizens *call* it informally?
3. Where exactly is it located? (building name, floor, room or counter number, ward, district)
4. **(Photo)** Take a picture of the front of the building, ideally with any signage visible.
5. Which areas does this office serve? (single ward, whole municipality, whole district, nationwide?)
6. Is there an alternative office for this service? (e.g., for people who live far)

---

## PART 2 — Services offered (5 min, repeat per service)

For each service this office handles (citizenship, passport, PAN, etc.):

7. What's the name of this service in Nepali / Roman-Nepali / English?
8. **Required documents — official list.** What does the gov.np website say is needed?
9. **Required documents — actual reality.** Is there anything you also ask for that isn't on that list? (Common gotchas: utility bills, recent photo, guarantor, etc.)
10. **Photos** — any specific size, color, count, background requirement? Where do people usually get them taken if they forgot?
11. **Forms** — where does the citizen get the form? Is it free, paid, or in advance from a printer? Common mistakes when filling?

---

## PART 3 — Process flow (5 min, per service)

12. When a citizen walks in for [service], **what's the first counter or room they go to**? What does that step do?
13. Then where? Walk me through the sequence.
14. About how long does each step take on a normal day?
15. How long on a *busy* day?
16. What's the most common reason someone gets sent back to a previous step?

---

## PART 4 — Logistics (3 min)

17. Office hours — official vs reality. When does the office actually start/end? Is there a lunch break that closes service?
18. **Best time of day to come?** Worst time?
19. **Worst days of the week / month / year**? (e.g., end-of-fiscal-year for tax offices)
20. **Parking** — where? How many spaces? Is it free?
21. **Public transport** — nearest stop, walking distance, what to look for?
22. **Inside the office** — bathroom, drinking water, seating, charging, AC?
23. **Nearby essentials** — closest photocopy shop, photo studio, ATM, food/tea?

---

## PART 5 — Practical tips (3 min)

24. What are the **top three reasons** people get rejected or sent away from this office?
25. If a citizen forgot something important (photo, photocopy, cash), is there a backup option nearby?
26. How do you handle documents in a foreign language? (passport copies, etc.)
27. Are there special arrangements for: minors? elderly? disabled? non-resident Nepalis?

---

## PART 6 — Cost reality (2 min)

28. What's the **official fee** for this service?
29. What other costs do citizens typically pay? (form, photo, photocopy, stamp, notary)
30. Can someone **expedite** the service if they're in a hurry? Officially or unofficially? (Don't name people; we just want the structural reality.)
31. Are there cost differences between the online vs in-person path?

---

## PART 7 — The "did you bring X?" gotchas (3 min)

32. What questions do officers commonly ask citizens that **aren't on any official list**? (E.g., "where's your utility bill?", "where's your guarantor?")
33. Has any of this changed in the last year? Any new requirements?

---

## PART 8 — Special cases (2 min)

34. Foreign citizen / NRN — different process?
35. Children / minors — what extra is needed?
36. Lost or damaged original document — what's the process?
37. Death certificate / inheritance / guardianship cases — anything unique?

---

## PART 9 — Recent changes (2 min)

38. Was there anything about this office that worked differently a year ago?
39. Are there any new policies or systems being introduced that aren't fully active yet?

---

## PART 10 — Free-form (2 min)

40. **Anything else** a first-time visitor should know that we haven't covered?
41. **What is the single most common mistake** you see citizens make at this office?

---

## PART 11 — Visual layer (during walk-through, no audio needed)

After the sit-down interview, ask if you can walk through the office briefly. Take photos of:

- Each counter/room with its label (Devanagari + English if visible)
- The flow path: front entrance → first counter → next → next
- Any board/notice that lists fees, hours, required documents
- Nearby photocopy/photo shop frontage (with name visible)
- Parking area
- Bathrooms (or sign indicating where)
- Building exterior with the official sign

These photos get associated with the interview during processing, indexed alongside the textual claims, and used by the mobile app's "navigate" feature.

---

## Post-interview (1 min)

42. Thank the interviewee.
43. Ask: "Is there anyone else here who'd be helpful to talk to about this?"
44. Note their answer to #43 in your fieldworker journal.
45. End recording. Save audio with the prescribed filename. Back up to cloud before leaving.

## Appendix A — Consent statement (English)

> "Your participation is voluntary. The information you provide will be used to build a public knowledge system that helps citizens use government services. Your name and any specific names you mention will be anonymized — only practical information about the office will be retained. The recording will be deleted after transcription and review. You may withdraw consent at any time, including after the interview, by contacting [project email]."

- [ ] Phone with camera
- [ ] Project email + WhatsApp number written legibly
