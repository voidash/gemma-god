Three weeks. Four offices. Four versions of the same form. Almost eight
thousand rupees to middlemen.

I was not paying for the government service. I was paying for the map.

The law was public. The route was privileged.

I could keep pushing because I had privilege: my sister helping with voice
data, GPU access, technical knowledge, and the stubbornness to keep breaking
the system until it moved.

Then I travelled 180 kilometers to Jiri to understand the grounded reality of a
real office. Because Kathmandu is also a privilege.

In Jiri, the gap became clearer. People may not read government websites, but
they already know how to call, speak, scroll, watch videos, and use WhatsApp.
So this was not only an information problem. It was a user-interface problem.

Gemma was pivotal because this could not just be a cloud chatbot. Government
help should work at the counter, on a kiosk, on WhatsApp, and inside the office
itself.

But the answer is not plain Gemma. The answer is PreVillage: a government
service navigator built around Gemma.

A normal chatbot guesses. PreVillage does intake. It asks what service, which
ward or district, which office, which document, which room, and which case.
Then it retrieves.

Behind that is the real infrastructure: government directories, PDFs, contact
pages, source registry, periodic crawls, health checks, and a self-healing RAG
pipeline that knows which source matters for which question.

RAG alone is still weak, because the most useful office knowledge is often
practical: which counter, which time, which document people forget, who
actually knows the answer.

So PreVillage has a tight human loop. When the system sees a real gap, it does
not hallucinate. It finds the relevant contact officer from official sources,
opens a direct WhatsApp conversation, and turns the answer back into reviewed
practical knowledge.

We also trained the voice layer for this. Nepali ASR, and Nepali TTS with Piper
and VITS, including a real voice collected at home. Voice is not decoration.
It is how this becomes usable for people who do not want another website.

A citizen speaks. ASR transcribes. Gemma fixes the messy text and plans the
question. RAG retrieves official and practical sources. If the source is not
enough, the human loop asks the officer. Then TTS speaks back, and WhatsApp
keeps the same help where people already talk.

The point is not that every office needs an L40 GPU. The heavy work builds the
knowledge. A Raspberry Pi or office computer can run a capable local helpdesk
onsite, close to the citizen and close to the data.

I used privilege to find the path. PreVillage exists so the next person does
not need privilege to use their own government.
